ABOUT ME

Om Kolhe

I am a PhD candidate at Purdue University, working under the mentorship of Prof. Krishna Jayant in the Nano Neurotechnology Lab. My research focuses on understanding the neural mechanisms of learning and decision-making by developing novel technologies and experimental protocols. I am particularly interested in how neurons communicate and coordinate across multiple modalities to perform computations that are both robust and plastic. To support this investigation, I developed a custom neural recording platform that integrates advanced electrode designs with a CMOS backend for multimodal interrogation of neural activity.

I earned a dual degree (B.Tech + M.Tech) in Electrical Engineering from the Indian Institute of Technology (IIT) Bombay in August 2020. For my Master’s thesis, I worked with Prof. Maryam Shojaei Baghini to design a multi-phase oscillator for next-generation 5G receivers. During my undergraduate studies, I worked under Prof. Shalabh Gupta on the front-end design of an IRNSS receiver (the Indian regional navigation satellite system).

RESEARCH PROJECTS

Spiking Neural Network Simulation

Developed large-scale simulations of spiking neural networks using biologically inspired neuron models to understand how networks of neurons encode and process information. Explored traveling waves and synaptic plasticity mechanisms.

Multimodal Neural Recording Platform

Designed a custom implantable platform integrating transparent ECoG arrays and two-photon imaging to simultaneously capture electrical and optical neural activity in behaving mice, enabling unprecedented multimodal analysis.

Traveling Wave Analysis in Cortical Circuits

Studied spatiotemporal patterns of activity propagating across the cortex, quantifying traveling wave properties during sensory processing and learning using phase gradient analysis on wideband LFP recordings.

Closed-Loop Neural Modulation

Implemented real-time signal processing for activity-dependent stimulation to investigate how closed-loop feedback influences circuit dynamics and behavioral performance.

Flexible Neural Probe Design

Developed ultra-flexible Parylene-based electrodes with improved biocompatibility and spatial resolution for chronic implantation and recording.
